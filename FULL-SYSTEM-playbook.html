Section 1: What is OpenClaw & Installation

OpenClaw in Plain English

OpenClaw is like having a team of interns who never sleep, never complain, and work for pennies. It's software that runs AI agents ‚Äî autonomous programs that can research, write, code, analyze data, and make decisions on your behalf.

Think of it this way: ChatGPT is a calculator. OpenClaw is a factory full of calculators that can run themselves.

What OpenClaw Actually Does

  • Automate repetitive tasks: Instead of spending 2 hours every morning answering the same customer emails, you teach an agent your responses once, and it handles them forever.
  • Research at scale: Need to check what 50 competitors are doing? An agent can visit all their websites, extract the key info, and give you a summary in minutes instead of days.
  • Generate content: Draft social media posts, blog articles, email newsletters ‚Äî all on a schedule you set, without you touching a keyboard.
  • Monitor and alert: Watch for specific things (price drops, news mentions, competitor moves) and ping you when something important happens.

Requirements

  • Computer running Windows, Mac, or Linux
  • Node.js installed (version 18+)
  • API keys for your chosen AI models (OpenAI, Anthropic, etc.)
  • Basic command line comfort (I'll walk you through everything)

Section 2: Use Cases for Your Business

Finding Your First Win

The key to successfully using OpenClaw isn't trying to automate everything at once. It's finding one repetitive task that eats up your time and automating that first. Get a quick win, build confidence, then expand.

Customer Support Automation

The Problem

You're answering the same questions repeatedly:

  • "What's your pricing?"
  • "How do I reset my password?"
  • "Do you offer refunds?"
  • "What are your hours?"

Each email takes 5 minutes. Twenty emails = nearly 2 hours. Every day.

The OpenClaw Solution

Level 1: Draft Responses
Agent monitors your support inbox. When emails arrive, it drafts responses based on your previous replies. You review and send. Cuts response time from 5 minutes to 30 seconds.

Level 2: Auto-Respond
Agent categorizes emails (simple question, complex issue, complaint, refund request). Handles simple questions automatically. Routes complex ones to you with context and suggested responses.

Level 3: Full Integration
Agent connects to your knowledge base, CRM, and order system. Looks up customer history before responding. Escalates only when it can't find the answer or detects angry sentiment.

I tried to build a Level 3 agent on day 3. Don't do that. Start with Level 1. Get it working. Then add complexity. I spent 2 days debugging a system that was too complex because I got ambitious.

Social Media Content Automation

The Problem

You know you should post daily. You have ideas. But sitting down to write, format, and schedule takes 30-45 minutes per post. So you do it inconsistently. Your social presence dies while you serve clients.

The OpenClaw Solution

Content Generation Agent:

  1. Reads your blog, website, past social posts to learn your voice
  2. Generates 7 days of content based on your topics
  3. Writes platform-optimized versions (Twitter vs. LinkedIn vs. Instagram)
  4. Schedules them in your social media tool
  5. Sends you a summary for review

Time saved: 5-7 hours per week
Result: Consistent presence without the daily grind

Token Management

Understanding Token Costs

API calls are charged by tokens (roughly 1 token = 0.75 words). Different models cost different amounts:

Model Input Cost (per 1K tokens) Output Cost (per 1K tokens)
GPT-4 $0.03 $0.06
GPT-4 Turbo $0.01 $0.03
GPT-3.5 $0.0005 $0.0015
Claude 3 $0.008 $0.024
‚ö†Ô∏è The $200 Mistake I Made:
I built an agent that monitored 50 RSS feeds every 10 minutes using GPT-4. That was 7,200 API calls per day. At $0.03 per call, that's $216/day. I turned it on and went to sleep. Woke up to a $200+ bill. Always calculate costs BEFORE deploying. Use GPT-3.5 for monitoring, GPT-4 only for final processing.

Cost Optimization Strategies

  1. Use the right model for the job: GPT-3.5 for simple tasks, GPT-4 only when needed
  2. Batch operations: Process 10 items in one prompt instead of 10 separate prompts
  3. Set rate limits: Configure OpenClaw to max out at $X per day
  4. Monitor usage weekly: I check my dashboard every Sunday to catch anomalies
  5. Use caching: Don't re-process the same data repeatedly
